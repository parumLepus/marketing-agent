FRAMEWORK: AIDA

Attention → Interest → Desire → Action
Use for: sales emails, landing pages, ad copy
Example: Get attention with a bold claim, build interest with data, create desire with benefits, close with a clear CTA.

FRAMEWORK: PAS

Problem → Agitation → Solution
Use for: LinkedIn posts, email openers, blog intros
Example: “Leads aren't converting (Problem). You're wasting budget on the wrong audience (Agitation). Here's a targeting fix that worked for 50+ B2B teams (Solution).”

FRAMEWORK: StoryBrand

Position the customer as the hero, your brand as the guide.
Use for: website copy, pitch decks, brand messaging
Structure: customer has a problem → meets a guide → gets a plan → takes action → avoids failure → achieves success.

FRAMEWORK: TOFU–MOFU–BOFU

Top → Middle → Bottom of Funnel
Use for: content strategy, funnel planning

TOFU: awareness content (education, virality)
MOFU: trust-building (case studies, comparisons)
BOFU: conversion content (offers, demos, CTAs)
FRAMEWORK: BAB (Before–After–Bridge)

Before → After → Bridge
Use for: landing pages, ads, email marketing

Before: current painful situation
After: desired transformation
Bridge: how your solution gets them there
FRAMEWORK: AARRR Funnel

Acquisition → Activation → Retention → Revenue → Referral
Use for: growth strategy, SaaS, product marketing
Focus: user journey optimisation and lifecycle growth

FRAMEWORK: Hook–Story–Offer

Hook → Story → Offer
Use for: social media, video scripts, content marketing

Hook: attention-grabbing statement
Story: relatable narrative or insight
Offer: CTA or conversion point
FRAMEWORK: 4P Marketing Mix

Product → Price → Place → Promotion
Use for: go-to-market strategy and positioning
Defines how a product is built, priced, distributed, and marketed.

FRAMEWORK: 4U HEADLINE FORMULA

Urgent → Useful → Unique → Ultra-specific
Use for: headlines, ads, hooks
Example: strong attention + clear benefit + specificity = higher CTR

FRAMEWORK: BLUE OCEAN STRATEGY

Compete → Differentiate → Create new demand
Use for: positioning strategy
Goal: avoid saturated markets and create new categories

FRAMEWORK: USP STACK

Unique → Specific → Provable
Use for: messaging and positioning clarity
A strong USP must be differentiated, measurable, and believable

FRAMEWORK: CONTENT PILLAR SYSTEM

Educational → Authority → Conversion → Engagement
Use for: content planning and social media strategy
Ensures balanced and scalable content output
